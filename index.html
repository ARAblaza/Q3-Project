OPEN FLAMES INDOORS

What else could cause a fire other than a smaller open fire? Open flames are naturally pretty obvious culprits for house fires.

DON'T LEAVE OPEN FLAMES UNATTENDED KEEP THEM AWAY FROM FLAMMABLE ITEMS
Although the tiny flames from candles look harmless, you should never leave it alone for too long. The candle could be knocked over or the flame could be blown in the direction of things prone to catching fire. As much as possible, keep a safe distance between open flames and fabrics like curtains and table cloths. Also be careful when handling the candle; roll up your sleeves and keep hair away from the flame.

2

IRRESPONSIBLE USAGE OF APPLIANCES

A terrifying amount of household items could just combust into a house fire at any time. This vulnerability is highlighted when these appliances aren't handled properly.

SPILLS ON ELECTRICAL EQUIPMENT DON'T LEAVE APPLIANCES UNPLUGGED CLEAN YOUR APPLIANCES
Make sure to keep liquids away from electrical equipment. If you feel as if Netflix just isn't the same without a glass of milk, make sure to be wary of spillage and dry it up immediately. As tedious as it can be to set up the toaster just for one piece of bread, never leave it plugged. At any point in time, the appliance may overheat or experience a power surge that could light fire to its surroundings. Dust and other dirt build-up can leave appliances prone to overheating. Be sure to perform regular maintenance around household to make sure nothing is getting too hot.

3

BEING A BAD COOK

Although we've already covered appliances, the infamous stove and oven require a category of its own. Most of the danger around cooking appliances boils down to leaving it unattended, but they are especially vulnerable because they are literally built to produce fire, and without proper care, it could easily spread well beyond the kitchen.

KEEP CHILDREN AND PETS AWAY ROLL UP YOUR SHIRTSLEEVES KEEP METAL AWAY FROM THE MICROWAVE, PLEASE
One happy-go-lucky cat is all it takes to turn on the stove while no one is looking. To ensure that stoves don't quietly transform into a house fire, keep kids and animals away. If you prefer your clothes without a burning inferno, wear short/tight sleeves or just roll them up so that they don't accidentally hang over the stove burners and catch fire. Okay, not technically a cooking appliance but it's an honourable mention nonetheless. Microwaving metal and tinfoil can have explosive and possibly house-burning-down results. Always make sure you didn't leave in your fork and always transfer tinfoil-wrapped food onto a plate.
